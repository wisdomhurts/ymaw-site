FAQ

Questions parents ask.

We know entrusting your son to a weekend program is a big decision. Here are answers to the questions we hear most often.

Safety & Supervision
Is my son safe at YMAW?

Safety is our top priority. All activities are supervised by trained leaders skilled in facilitating group dynamics. Every activity is age-appropriate, carefully planned, and risk-assessed. We maintain appropriate adult-to-participant ratios and have first aid–certified team members on site at all times.

Who are the leaders and volunteers?

Our production team consists of dedicated men who volunteer their time to positively influence younger men. All volunteers undergo screening and receive training on YMAW's approach to working with teenage boys, group facilitation, and safety protocols before the weekend.

What happens if my son gets injured or sick?

We have first aid–certified team members on site and a clear emergency response plan. Parents provide emergency contact and medical information during registration. We will contact you immediately if any concern arises regarding your son's health or safety.

Can I contact my son during the weekend?

Part of the YMAW experience is disconnecting from screens and distractions. We encourage participants to leave phones at home. However, in case of emergency, parents can reach the production team at any time through a dedicated contact number provided before the weekend.

Logistics
What ages is YMAW for?

YMAW is designed for teenage boys ages 12–17. Activities are structured so participants can learn and grow at their own pace, regardless of fitness level or prior outdoor experience.

Where does YMAW take place?

YMAW takes place in the Vancouver, BC area. The exact location is shared with registered families prior to the weekend. The venue is selected for its natural beauty, safety, and suitability for our programming.

What are the dates?

The next YMAW is scheduled for July 11–13, 2025. Participants arrive Friday evening by 5pm and depart Sunday after lunch. Subscribe to our mailing list for updates on future dates.

How much does it cost?

Registration is $279 CAD. This covers all meals (Friday dinner through Sunday lunch), all activities and equipment, trained leader supervision, and camping facilities. The only additional cost is transportation to and from the venue.

Is financial assistance available?

Yes. We believe every young man should have the opportunity to attend YMAW regardless of financial circumstances. Scholarships and financial assistance are available for families in need. Please contact us to learn more — all inquiries are confidential.

Preparation
What should my son bring?

A detailed packing list is provided upon registration. Essentials include: a sleeping bag, weather-appropriate clothing (layers recommended), sturdy hiking boots or trail shoes, a water bottle, sunscreen, a rain jacket, and personal hygiene items. All activity gear is provided by YMAW.

Does my son need outdoor experience?

No prior outdoor experience is required. YMAW is designed to be inclusive of all skill and fitness levels. Activities are structured so every participant can learn and grow at their own pace. Our trained leaders provide instruction and support throughout.

My son is shy / anxious — is YMAW right for him?

Often, these are the boys who benefit most. YMAW's supportive environment is specifically designed to help young men step outside their comfort zone safely. Our trained facilitators are skilled at drawing out quieter participants without pressuring them. Many parents report their son returned more confident and connected.

Can my son attend with a friend?

Absolutely. While we encourage participants to make new connections, attending with a friend can help ease any initial nerves. Just let us know during registration and we'll make sure they're in the same group.

Still have questions?

We're happy to talk. Reach out and a member of our team will get back to you.
