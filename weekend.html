The Weekend

Challenging, engaging, unforgettable.

Three days of hands-on adventure, leadership development, and real connection. Here's what your son can expect.

Overview

A weekend built with purpose.

Every activity at YMAW is designed with intention. Our program blends outdoor adventure with leadership development, creating an environment where teenage boys are challenged to push their limits, work as a team, and discover what they're truly capable of.

Participants disconnect from screens and other distractions and immerse themselves in nature, mentorship, and shared experience. Activities are age-appropriate and structured so every young man can learn and grow at their own pace.

The Schedule

Three days, one journey.

Day 1

Friday — Arrival & Opening

5:00 PM Arrival and check-in. Meet the production team and fellow participants.
6:30 PM Welcome dinner and group introductions.
8:00 PM Opening ceremony and team formation. Setting intentions for the weekend.
Day 2

Saturday — The Challenge

7:00 AM Sunrise and breakfast. Prepare for the day ahead.
8:30 AM Morning activities: hiking, orienteering, and outdoor skills workshops.
12:30 PM Lunch and rest period.
2:00 PM Afternoon challenges: team problem-solving, leadership exercises, and adventure activities.
7:00 PM Dinner and campfire. Stories, reflection, and community.
Day 3

Sunday — Closing & Departure

7:30 AM Breakfast and morning reflection.
9:00 AM Final group activity and closing ceremony.
12:00 PM Lunch and departure. Head home with new skills, new friends, and a new perspective.
Activities

What we do.

01

Hiking & Orienteering

Navigate backcountry trails, learn to read a compass and topographic map, and experience the beauty of BC's wilderness firsthand.

02

Leadership Challenges

Structured group exercises that build trust, communication, and decision-making. Designed to reveal leadership potential in every participant.

03

Camping & Bushcraft

Shelter building, fire craft, knot tying, and wilderness awareness. Practical outdoor skills taught hands-on by experienced leaders.

04

Team Problem Solving

Creative challenges that require collaboration, critical thinking, and persistence. Every participant contributes — no one sits on the sidelines.

05

Campfire & Reflection

Evening gatherings around the fire for stories, music, and honest conversation. The moments that turn a group of strangers into a crew.

06

Closing Ceremony

A meaningful wrap-up honoring each participant's journey over the weekend. A moment of recognition, gratitude, and looking forward.

What to Bring

Come prepared. We handle the rest.

A detailed packing list is provided upon registration. All activity gear, meals, and supervision are included in the registration fee. Here's the essentials:

Sleeping bag and sleeping pad
Weather-appropriate clothing (layers recommended)
Sturdy hiking boots or trail shoes
Water bottle and sunscreen
Rain jacket
Personal hygiene items
A willingness to try something new

What's included.

Your registration covers everything your son needs for the weekend:

All meals (Friday dinner through Sunday lunch)
All activity equipment and gear
Trained leader supervision throughout
Camping facilities
First aid and safety equipment
